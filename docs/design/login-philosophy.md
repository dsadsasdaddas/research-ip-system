# Citation Geometry

*A visual philosophy for the act of registration.*

---

## Manifesto

Knowledge is registered, indexed, and quietly preserved. The discipline of citation —
the precise notation of who-what-when — is itself a visual language. We honor that
discipline through restraint: a page that breathes, a single graphite mark on warm
paper, the typographic poise of a scientific reprint. The user does not arrive at a
"login screen" so much as the title block of their own publication — ready to be authored.

## Form and Space

The composition rests on a strict twelve-column grid, but only three or four columns
are ever occupied. The upper two-thirds of the page is given to silence — vast vertical
breathing room — so that the form sits low, the way an abstract sits beneath a title
on the cover of a printed journal. A single hairline rule, half a point thick, divides
metadata from inscription. Nothing is decorative. Every line earns its place through
purpose, and every margin is measured to the millimeter, as if the page were being set
in metal type by hand.

## Color and Material

The background is the warm off-white of acid-free archival paper — never pure white,
never gray, simply the color of a page that has been kept well. Graphite serves as the
primary ink. A single steel-blue accent, used once — perhaps as a thin underscore beneath
the institution mark, or as the verb of a quiet action — punctuates the composition.
There are no gradients, no shadows, no luminance effects: this is the texture of paper,
not screen. The palette is the product of painstaking restraint, the work of a craftsman
who has spent years removing things rather than adding them.

## Typography

Type is set small and deliberate. A thin serif (or a geometric sans cut to the same
discipline) carries the institution mark; uppercase, letter-spaced labels mark the
metadata fields the way they would be marked on a library catalog card. Each input is
not a box but an underscore — a thin line waiting for ink. There are no placeholders
shouting at the reader; there is only quiet expectation. The reading rhythm moves
top-to-bottom, like a journal masthead: institution, work, author, action. Every
letterform is chosen with the patience of someone who has cared about kerning for
a lifetime.

## Scale, Rhythm, Composition

Generous vertical rhythm, modular and patient: each element separated by a multiple
of a single base unit, so the page sings in a quiet meter. The institution mark sits
high; the form sits low; between them, nothing but air and one fine rule. Small
metadata in the corners — a date, a system designation, a serial — anchors the page
in the manner of an editorial colophon. The composition is balanced not by symmetry
but by weight: a careful, hand-tuned distribution that has been refined through
countless iterations until nothing further can be removed.

## Craftsmanship

The final work must read as though it were labored over for many hours — the kind of
restraint that only deep expertise produces. Every margin measured. Every rule precisely
0.5 points. Every alignment the result of patient, master-level refinement. The viewer
should sense, without being told, that this page is the product of someone at the top
of their field, working slowly, removing rather than adding, until only the essential
remains. This is not a design for attention; it is a design for trust.
